Heart

Traditional wedding gifts include paper heart origami, which is meant to wish the happy couple a thousand decades of happiness and success. Additionally, they can be given to a newborn for longer life and good fortune. It is said that hanging these in one's home acts as a powerful good luck charm. Each heart is made using a rectangle instead of a standard square piece of origami paper which makes them perfect for making out of A4 paper.


Step of Instructions

1. Take a A4 size piece of paper. You can also use a 15cm x 15cm piece of origami paper. Thinner paper is preferable as thicker paper is harder to work with and doesn't always stay folded.

2. Turn the paper to the side that is white. Then, fold the top right corner downwards so that it meets the left side of the paper. Unfold it, and do the same thing to the opposite corner; do not unfold.

3. Fold the bottom part of the paper in half. Do this so that the white or the inside part of the paper doesn't show anymore.

4. Unfold the top part of the paper. You should now have to diagonal creases on the paper.

5. Make a horizontal fold. Fold the top part down horizontally, so that the fold crosses the intersection in the middle of the paper. Then, unfold it.

6. Turn the paper over again. Take the left and right sides of the paper where the horizontal crease is and pull them toward the center of the paper. As you pull them, the other creases should be folding, too. Pull the two sides inwards so they touch.

7. Fold the bottom left corner of the top triangle so it meets the top point. Only fold the top layer, not both layers of paper. Make the same fold on the other side; you should now have a diamond shape.

8. Fold over the sides to meet the diamond. Grab the left side of the paper, and fold everything that is not part of the diamond you made in the previous step toward the center. Do the same thing for the other side.

9. Create a vertical crease. Fold the entire thing half vertically, then unfold it and turn it over.

10. Fold up the bottom corners. Take the two bottom corners and fold them upwards, so they meet at the center. Fold them so that what was once the bottom edge lines up with the vertical line running down the center.

11. Fold down the top flap. Fold the large triangular flap at the top downward, as far as it can go before running into a horizontal line. At the top there should now be three individual flaps, two small ones and a big one. Fold the big one down.

12. Tuck the corners. Tuck the two corners that you folded up from the bottom into the space inside the triangular flap.

13. Fold down the top points. Fold the two remaining top flaps downwards at an angle.

14. Tuck the corners again. Tuck the corners of those flaps into the space in the big flap.

15. Check the finished heart. You should now have an origami heart.
